NVDA Nvidia Corporation
Conviction High
Current Thesis

Nvidia's moat is not the GPU — it is CUDA and the decade-long developer lock-in it represents. As long as hyperscalers are in a race to deploy AI capacity, Nvidia captures a disproportionate share of that spend. Heading into Q1 FY2026, the headline EPS number matters less than I thought six months ago. The real question is Blackwell revenue mix and what management says about H2 visibility. Here is what I am specifically watching:

Blackwell revenue as a percentage of data centre revenue — if it's below 60% that's a yellow flag on the ramp timeline.
Gross margin trajectory. Blackwell initially came in below Hopper margins. I want to see the guidance imply a recovery path.
Any colour on China — export restrictions have created a hole in revenue that management keeps downplaying. I want to hear specifics on alternative product lines.
Hyperscaler capex commentary from the call, cross-referenced against what Microsoft and Google said in their own earnings.

My base case is a beat on revenue with in-line guidance. A raised H2 outlook would push my conviction to very high. A cautious tone on China or gross margins would make me revisit position sizing.

Devil's Advocate The bear case on Nvidia I take seriously
11 Apr 2026 Copy link
↓

I am long Nvidia and my conviction is high. But periodically I try to steelman the bear case as honestly as I can — because the best way to know if your thesis is intact is to challenge it seriously.

Hyperscaler capex is not unlimited. Microsoft, Google, and Amazon are all building custom silicon precisely because they don't want permanent GPU dependency. TPUs, Trainium, and Maia are immature now — but the trajectory is real.
CUDA moat may be overstated. The ecosystem is real, but PyTorch has abstracted a significant portion of the stack. A determined hyperscaler can port workloads.
Valuation assumes sustained AI infrastructure growth. If AI model economics worsen — if inference becomes commoditised faster than expected — capex freezes fast.

None of these are reasons to sell for me right now. But they are the flags I am watching. If hyperscaler capex guidance starts softening in back-to-back quarters, that is the moment I reduce exposure regardless of near-term earnings.

MU Micron Technology
Conviction Medium
Current Thesis

Micron is a cyclical bet with a structural overlay. The cyclical: DRAM is recovering from a brutal oversupply trough and pricing is firming. The structural: HBM demand from AI accelerators is real and Micron is one of three suppliers in a duopoly-plus-one market. My medium conviction reflects the cyclical risk — if PC and smartphone demand weakens again, conventional DRAM pricing softens quickly. The HBM story alone doesn't carry the whole thesis.

News Reaction On the tariff risk and what it means for Micron specifically
10 Apr 2026 Copy link
↓

Tariff escalation is the dominant macro risk right now. For Micron specifically, the exposure is real but nuanced. Micron manufactures in the US (Virginia, Idaho) and Japan — less China-exposed than most assume. The bigger tariff risk for Micron is indirect: if tariffs slow PC and smartphone demand in Asia, conventional DRAM pricing weakens and the cyclical recovery thesis gets pushed out by 2–3 quarters. My position is unchanged but I'm sizing carefully here rather than adding.

TSM Taiwan Semiconductor
Conviction High
Current Thesis

TSMC is the toll road of the semiconductor industry. Every chip designed by Nvidia, Apple, AMD, or Qualcomm gets manufactured here. The geopolitical risk is real and permanently discounts the stock — but that discount is the opportunity. Advanced packaging (CoWoS) is the current bottleneck for HBM and Nvidia's GPU supply chain, and TSMC controls it. Arizona fabs reduce — but don't eliminate — the Taiwan risk premium.
